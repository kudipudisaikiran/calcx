How EMI is Calculated

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is calculated using the reducing balance method. Each month, interest is charged on the outstanding principal, and the rest of the EMI goes toward reducing the principal.

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ - 1)

Where P = Principal, r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = Total months. A higher tenure reduces the EMI but increases total interest paid.

How SIP Returns are Calculated

SIP uses compound interest on each installment. Each monthly investment earns returns for the remaining period, and since investments are spread over time, the average holding period is roughly half the total tenure.

M = P × [(1 + i)ⁿ - 1] / i × (1 + i)

Where P = monthly investment, i = monthly rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = total months. SIP is one of the most disciplined ways to build long-term wealth through rupee cost averaging.

The Magic of Compounding

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest. Unlike simple interest, compounding earns "interest on interest," making it exponentially powerful over long periods.

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

Where P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency per year, t = time in years. More frequent compounding (daily vs annually) yields slightly higher returns.

Understanding Your Mortgage

A mortgage is a home loan where your property serves as collateral. The monthly payment includes both principal repayment and interest. In early years, most of the payment goes toward interest; gradually, more goes toward principal (amortization).

A larger down payment reduces the loan amount, lowers monthly payments, and significantly reduces total interest paid over the loan tenure.

Indian Income Tax Regimes

New Regime (Default from FY 2023-24): Standard deduction of ₹75,000. Tax slabs: 0% up to ₹3L, 5% (₹3L–7L), 10% (₹7L–10L), 15% (₹10L–12L), 20% (₹12L–15L), 30% above ₹15L. Rebate u/s 87A for income up to ₹7L.

Old Regime: Allows deductions like 80C (₹1.5L), HRA, 80D etc. Beneficial if you have significant investments/deductions. Slabs: 0% up to ₹2.5L, 5% (₹2.5L–5L), 20% (₹5L–10L), 30% above ₹10L. A 4% Health & Education cess applies on total tax.

Understanding BMI

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. While it's a useful screening tool, it doesn't directly measure body fat or account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution.

BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)²

Categories: Underweight (<18.5), Normal (18.5–24.9), Overweight (25–29.9), Obese Class I (30–34.9), Obese Class II (35–39.9), Obese Class III (≥40). Athletes may have higher BMI due to muscle mass without being unhealthy.

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most accurate formula for estimating BMR (the calories your body burns at rest). The TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) multiplies BMR by an activity factor.

Male: BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H - 5×A + 5
Female: BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H - 5×A - 161

To lose weight, eat ~500 kcal less than maintenance (loses ~0.5kg/week). To gain weight, eat ~500 kcal more. Never go below 1200 kcal/day (women) or 1500 kcal/day (men) without medical supervision.

Naegele's Rule

The estimated due date (EDD) is calculated using Naegele's Rule: add 40 weeks (280 days) to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). This is adjusted for cycles longer or shorter than 28 days.

Pregnancy is divided into three trimesters: First (weeks 1–12), Second (weeks 13–26), and Third (weeks 27–40). The due date is an estimate — only 4% of babies are born on their exact due date, with most arriving within 2 weeks before or after.

About the Formulas

Each formula was developed by different researchers and may give slightly different results. They are all based on height and gender, using the concept of "ideal" weight for health outcomes.

Robinson (1983) and Miller (1983) are modern refinements. Devine (1974) is widely used in pharmaceutical dosing. Hamwi (1964) is the oldest and most conservative. Use these as general guidelines, not strict targets — body composition, muscle mass, and bone density all matter.
